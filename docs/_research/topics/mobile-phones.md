# Research Memo: Mobile Phones

## Question
What are the most historically significant mobile phones from 1983–2007, and what sources document them authoritatively?

## Key Facts

- The Motorola DynaTAC 8000X (1983) was the first commercially available handheld cellular phone. It weighed 1.75 lbs, cost $3,995, and offered 30 minutes of talk time. (Source: Smithsonian)
- The Nokia 3310 (2000) sold over 126 million units globally. Its durability became a cultural meme, and it represented the peak of the feature phone era. (Source: Computer History Museum)
- The Motorola Razr V3 (2004) was a design milestone — a thin, metallic flip phone that sold 130 million units and signaled that phones were fashion objects. (Source: Smithsonian)
- The Apple iPhone (2007) combined a phone, iPod, and internet communicator. It introduced the capacitive touchscreen and established the smartphone as the dominant computing device. (Source: Computer History Museum)
- Global mobile phone subscribers reached 1 billion in 2002, up from 11 million in 1990. (Source: Britannica)
