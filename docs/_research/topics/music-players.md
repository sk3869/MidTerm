# Research Memo: Music Players

## Question
What are the most historically significant portable music players from 1979–2004, and what sources document them authoritatively?

## Key Facts

- Sony introduced the Walkman TPS-L2 in 1979. It created the category of personal portable music and changed how people experienced public space. 400 million units were sold over its lifetime. (Source: Smithsonian)
- The Discman (CD Walkman) launched in 1984, introducing digital audio to portable listening at a consumer price point. (Source: Computer History Museum)
- Diamond Multimedia released the Rio PMP300 in 1998, one of the first portable MP3 players. The Recording Industry Association of America immediately sued to block it — and lost. (Source: Britannica)
- Apple released the iPod in 2001 with a 5GB hard drive holding 1,000 songs. Its scroll wheel interface and iTunes integration created a closed but seamless ecosystem. (Source: Computer History Museum)
- By 2004, the iPod accounted for 92% of the hard-drive based music player market in the US. (Source: Smithsonian)
