# Research Memo: Personal Computers

## Question
What are the most historically significant personal computers from the 1970s–1990s, and what sources document them authoritatively?

## Key Facts

- The Altair 8800 (1975) is widely considered the first commercially successful personal computer. It was sold as a kit for $439. (Source: Computer History Museum)
- Apple's Steve Wozniak designed the Apple I in 1976 as a fully assembled board. The Apple II (1977) was the first mass-market success with color graphics. (Source: Computer History Museum)
- IBM's introduction of the IBM PC in 1981 established the open-architecture standard. By licensing MS-DOS and using off-the-shelf parts, IBM unintentionally created a clone market. (Source: Britannica)
- The Macintosh (1984) introduced the graphical user interface and mouse to mass-market consumers, popularizing concepts developed at Xerox PARC. (Source: Britannica)
- By 1990, approximately 54 million PCs were in use in the United States. (Source: historytools.org)
