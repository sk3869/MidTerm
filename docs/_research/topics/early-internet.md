# Research Memo: Early Internet

## Question
What are the most significant milestones and artifacts of the consumer internet from 1991–2004?

## Key Facts

- Tim Berners-Lee published the first website on August 6, 1991. The World Wide Web became publicly available and was distinct from the internet — it was a service running on top of the internet. (Source: Computer History Museum)
- NCSA Mosaic (1993) was the first web browser to display inline images alongside text. It made the web visually accessible to non-technical users and triggered mainstream adoption. (Source: Britannica)
- AOL's CD distribution campaign (1993–2000) mailed over 1 billion CDs to American households. At its peak, AOL had 35 million subscribers and represented the majority of U.S. internet users. (Source: Smithsonian)
- Google launched in 1998, replacing directory-based navigation (Yahoo, DMOZ) with algorithmic search. It fundamentally changed how people accessed information on the web. (Source: Computer History Museum)
- Broadband internet (DSL and cable) began replacing dial-up in 2000–2004. By 2004, broadband subscribers in the US outnumbered dial-up subscribers for the first time. (Source: Britannica)

## Primary Artifacts Identified

| Name | Year | Significance |
|---|---|---|
| NCSA Mosaic Browser | 1993 | First graphical web browser; made web accessible to general public |
| AOL Dial-Up Service | 1993 | Introduced email and online communities to mainstream America |
| Netscape Navigator | 1994 | Dominated early web browsing; drove the first dot-com investment wave |
| Google Search | 1998 | Replaced directory navigation; defined modern information access |
